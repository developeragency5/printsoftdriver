Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how PrintSoftDriver (www.printsoftdriver.com) uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our Site. This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy, which provides further detail on how we handle personal information collected through cookies. By using our Site and accepting our cookie consent banner, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used by website operators to make sites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences between visits, and to provide information about how visitors use the site. Cookies typically contain the name of the website they come from, the duration for which the cookie will be stored on your device, and a value — usually a randomly generated unique identifier.

Cookies may be "session cookies" which are deleted when you close your browser, or "persistent cookies" which remain on your device until they expire or until you delete them. Different cookies serve different purposes, ranging from essential site functionality to analytics and advertising. You have the right to control which non-essential cookies are stored on your device.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential / Functional Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Site to function correctly. They include the cookie consent preference stored in your browser's localStorage (under the key "cookieConsent") that remembers whether you have accepted or rejected cookies. Without this cookie, the consent banner would reappear on every page load. These cookies do not track your activity across other websites and are not used for any marketing purpose.

Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics cookies to collect aggregated, anonymized information about how visitors use our Site — which pages are visited most frequently, how long visitors spend on each page, and through which pages visitors typically enter and exit the Site. This information helps us improve the quality and relevance of our educational content. Analytics data is processed in aggregated form and does not identify individual visitors.

Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party advertising measurement and analytics technologies that rely on standard tracking pixels and cookies. These services may place cookies on your device to help us understand how users interact with our content and to measure the effectiveness of our educational resources. Such third-party services use this data in accordance with their own privacy practices. You can typically learn more and opt out of personalized advertising through your browser settings or industry opt-out tools.

Third-party cookies are governed by the respective privacy policies of those third parties. We do not control third-party cookies or the information they may collect. If you wish to prevent third-party cookies from being set, you can configure your browser to block all third-party cookies or use browser privacy extensions that block tracking scripts.

How to Control Cookies

You have several options for controlling cookies on our Site and across the web. First, you can use our cookie consent banner to reject all non-essential cookies when you first visit the Site. Second, you can configure your browser settings to block all cookies, block third-party cookies only, or prompt you before accepting any cookie — all major browsers provide these controls in their privacy and security settings menus. Third, you can delete cookies already stored on your device through your browser's settings at any time.

Please be aware that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our Site and other websites you visit. Rejecting cookies does not prevent you from reading our educational content, but it may affect how your preferences are remembered between sessions. For more detailed guidance on controlling cookies across popular browsers, you can visit www.allaboutcookies.org.
