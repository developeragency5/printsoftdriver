Printer Prints Blank or Garbled Pages

When perfectly fine documents come out of the printer as ghostly white sheets — or jumbled symbols that look like a robot sneezed on the page — the driver is almost always the culprit. Here is the calm, ordered fix.

Difficulty: Easy ~5–10 minutes No tools needed
Home › Troubleshooting › Printer Blank Pages
60-Second Quick Fix

Try This First — Solves It About Half the Time

  1. Power the printer fully off, wait twenty seconds, and switch it back on.
  2. Print a test page from the printer's own menu (not your computer) — that confirms whether the printer itself is healthy.
  3. Restart your computer, open the document, and try printing once more before changing anything else.
What's Actually Happening

Why Pages Come Out Blank or Garbled

When you hit Print, your application hands the document to your operating system, which hands it to the printer driver. The driver translates that document into a special instruction set the printer's tiny on-board computer can understand. If the driver is the wrong version, partially installed, or fighting with an old leftover driver, the translation breaks — and your printer either receives nothing it understands (blank pages) or receives jumbled instructions (garbled pages).

Blank pages also commonly appear when a printer is reporting full ink or toner internally but does not have enough to lay down a clean page. Garbled output, on the other hand, is almost always a driver-language mismatch — the printer is doing exactly what it was told, the trouble is what it was told was nonsense.

Either way, the fix is to reset the conversation between your computer and your printer. That means clearing out the printer queue, removing the old driver cleanly, and installing a fresh one straight from the manufacturer.

The Calm, Step-by-Step Fix

Reset the Printer–Computer Conversation

Work through these in order. Most readers solve the problem by step three — and almost everyone by step five.

1

Clear the Print Queue Completely

A single stuck print job can stall every job after it. Open the printer queue from your operating system's settings and cancel every pending document.

  • Open Settings → Devices → Printers (or the equivalent on your OS).
  • Click your printer, then open the queue.
  • Cancel every job listed, even ones marked as 'spooling' or 'error'.
2

Check Ink, Toner, and Paper Path

Before blaming software, do a five-second hardware check. Some printers print blank when one cartridge is dry — even if you only need black.

  • Open the printer and visually check every cartridge or toner level.
  • Run the printer's built-in nozzle clean cycle once.
  • Check the paper guide is firmly against the paper, not loose.
3

Uninstall the Existing Driver Cleanly

Old, corrupted driver files are the single biggest cause of garbled output. Removing them properly clears the slate.

  • Open Device Manager and find the printer under 'Print queues'.
  • Right-click the printer entry and choose Uninstall device.
  • When asked, tick the box to remove the driver software too.
  • Restart the computer fully — do not skip this.
4

Download the Latest Driver from the Maker

Always go straight to the printer manufacturer's official support page. Search for your exact model number and your operating system version.

  • Pick the full driver package, not the basic 'driver only' option.
  • Match the operating system version exactly — 64-bit and 32-bit are different.
  • Save the installer somewhere obvious like the Desktop.
5

Install Fresh, Reboot, Then Test

Run the freshly downloaded installer, follow the prompts, restart once more, and print a test page from the printer settings.

  • Right-click the installer and choose Run as administrator if available.
  • Accept the default install location unless you have a reason not to.
  • After install, restart again and print a test page from Printer Settings.
Common Root Causes

Why This Happens In the First Place

Tap any cause to read the friendly explanation behind it.

Why does my printer print perfectly white pages?
Either the cartridge is empty (even if the indicator says otherwise — printers can be optimistic), the print head is clogged, or the driver sent an empty command set. Run the cleaning cycle, then reinstall the driver.
Why are pages coming out with strange characters?
That is a classic driver-language mismatch. The printer is being sent instructions in a dialect it does not understand — usually because a generic driver was installed instead of the model-specific one.
Why did this only start after a system update?
Operating system updates sometimes replace your tuned printer driver with a basic generic one. Reinstalling the maker's driver restores the proper version.
Why does only certain software fail to print?
Some applications use their own print pipeline. If only one app is broken, try printing the same document to a PDF first, then printing the PDF — that often bypasses the issue.

What Not To Do

Avoid third-party "driver updater" tools that promise instant fixes. They often install bundles you do not need and occasionally cause the very problems they claim to solve. Stick to the official manufacturer support page for your device.

If It Still Will Not Cooperate

If you have worked through every step and the problem persists, the issue may be hardware, not software. Try the device on a second computer if you can. If it still fails, that is a strong sign the device itself is at fault.

A Calming Reminder

Almost every problem on this page has a fix that takes less time than making a cup of tea. Take a breath, work through the steps in order, and you will get there.
