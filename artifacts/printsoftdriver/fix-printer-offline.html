Fix: Printer Showing as Offline When It Is Clearly On

Windows and macOS sometimes mark a perfectly healthy printer as offline. A friendly walkthrough through the small steps that usually resolve it.

Home › Troubleshooting › Fix: Printer Showing Offline

Quick Wins

Restart both the printer and the computer. Yes, it is the cliché — but with print queues this remains the single most effective first step. Restarting clears stale jobs, refreshes the printer's network registration, and resets driver state on the computer.

If you are on Wi-Fi, ensure both the computer and the printer are on the same network. Many printers fall offline when the router hands them a new IP address — a router restart can sometimes flush this and let the printer rejoin cleanly.

  • Restart printer and computer
  • Confirm both are on the same Wi-Fi network
  • Restart the router if the printer is wireless

Use Printer Offline and Print Queue

Open Devices and Printers (Windows) or Printers & Scanners (macOS), find your printer, and check whether it has been manually set to Offline or Pause. Both flags will keep the printer dark even if everything else is fine.

Also clear the print queue — old stuck jobs sometimes block new ones. Right-click the printer, open the queue, and cancel everything pending.

Driver Reinstall

If the printer remains stubbornly offline, remove it entirely from your printer list and add it back from scratch. The fresh add-printer flow forces a re-discovery and a fresh driver install, which clears most lingering state issues.

On Windows, also restart the Print Spooler service from services.msc. A frozen spooler is a common cause of universal 'offline' messages across all printers.
