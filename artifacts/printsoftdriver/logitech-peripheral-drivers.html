Logitech Drivers

Logitech Drivers and the Optional G HUB / Options+ Story

How Logitech's drivers and companion software work together, and what to install if you only want the basics.

Home › Driver Guides › Logitech Drivers

What Logitech's Drivers Actually Do

Logitech splits its modern software into two main families. Logitech Options+ targets office mice and keyboards, while Logitech G HUB targets gaming gear. Both are technically optional — Windows' generic input drivers will run any Logitech mouse or keyboard at the basic level.

The companion app adds the interesting parts: per-application button mapping, lighting profiles, smart-shift behaviour on the high-end MX mice, and per-app DPI settings on G-series mice. None of these are needed for a device to type or click; they sit on top.

  • Options+ for productivity devices
  • G HUB for gaming devices
  • Older devices may still rely on the legacy Options or LGS apps

Optional Companion Apps and Their Footprint

Almost every modern device brand ships a companion app for lighting, button mapping, and macros. Logitech's app is one of these — it is not strictly required for the device to function as a basic mouse, keyboard, or headset, but it unlocks the more interesting customisation features.

If your priority is a lean install, you can use the basic Windows class driver and skip the companion app entirely. You lose macros and lighting but gain a much smaller resident footprint.

Updating Without the Companion App

Most Logitech devices receive firmware updates through their companion app. If you skip the app, firmware updates simply do not arrive — usually fine for years, but worth knowing in case a real fix appears.

If you eventually want a firmware update, install the app temporarily, apply the update, and uninstall it again. The device keeps the new firmware permanently.
