Privacy Policy

PrintSoftDriver ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you visit our website at www.printsoftdriver.com (the "Site"). Please read this policy carefully. If you disagree with its terms, please stop using the Site.

Information We Collect

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our Site, certain information is collected automatically by our web server and analytics systems. This includes your Internet Protocol (IP) address, browser type and version, operating system, the pages you visit on our Site, the time and date of your visit, the duration of time spent on each page, and the referring URL. This information is collected in aggregate and is generally not linked to any personally identifying information.

Information You Provide Voluntarily

If you contact us through our contact form, we collect the information you provide, including your name, email address, and the content of your message. This information is collected solely to respond to your inquiry and is not used for marketing purposes without your explicit consent.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to collect certain information automatically. See our Cookie Policy for detailed information.

How We Use Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes: to operate and maintain our Site and ensure it functions properly for all visitors; to understand how visitors interact with our content so we can improve its quality and relevance; to respond to inquiries submitted through our contact form; to monitor usage patterns to detect and prevent technical issues, security threats, or abuse; and to comply with applicable legal obligations.

We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise transfer your personally identifiable information to third parties for commercial purposes. We do not use your information for targeted advertising based on personal profiles, and we do not build individual user profiles from browsing data collected on this Site.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. We use cookies to remember your preferences (such as your cookie consent choice), to understand how visitors use our Site, and to improve performance and user experience.

We may use generic third-party analytics and advertising measurement services that rely on standard tracking pixels and cookies to help us understand how visitors interact with our content and to measure the effectiveness of our educational resources. These services may collect aggregated, non-identifying data about your visits using cookies and similar tracking technologies. For full details, please see our Cookie Policy.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or delete existing cookies. Note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some features on our Site.

Third-Party Services

Our Site may use third-party services to support its operation and help us understand how visitors use our content. These may include web analytics services and content delivery networks. These third parties may collect information about your use of our Site and other websites over time in accordance with their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you interact with through our Site.

We do not integrate social media share buttons, embedded social media feeds, or third-party advertising networks that track users across sites for targeted advertising based on personal profiles.

Data Retention

We retain automatically collected usage data in aggregated, anonymized form for a period of up to 24 months for the purpose of analyzing long-term trends in Site usage. Contact form submissions are retained for a maximum of 90 days after which they are permanently deleted, unless an ongoing correspondence requires retention for a longer period. Cookie preference data stored in your browser's localStorage is retained until you clear your browser data or until 12 months have elapsed, whichever occurs first.

Your Rights Under CCPA (California Users)

If you are a resident of California, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) regarding your personal information. These rights include: the right to know what personal information we collect about you; the right to know whether your personal information is sold or disclosed and to whom; the right to opt out of the sale of your personal information; the right to access the personal information we hold about you; and the right to request deletion of your personal information.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@printsoftdriver.com. We will respond to verified requests within 45 days as required by law. We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your rights under the CCPA.

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), California residents have the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of their personal information. We do not sell personal information. We do not share personal information with third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising purposes. To exercise your privacy rights or if you have any questions about how your data is handled, please contact us at info@printsoftdriver.com.

Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Our Site is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. We are committed to compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 13 without verifiable parental consent, we will take immediate steps to delete that information from our records. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at info@printsoftdriver.com so we can take appropriate action.

Security

We implement reasonable administrative and technical security measures to protect the limited personal information we collect. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. We encourage you to use caution when sharing personal information on any website.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information. Your continued use of the Site after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
